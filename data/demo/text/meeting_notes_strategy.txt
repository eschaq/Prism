1. Competitive landscape update

1. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
Lisa presented the competitive intelligence brief. Three new AI-native BI tools have entered the market in the last 6 months: Vanna.ai (raised $28M Series A), QueryMind (launched out of stealth with Google Ventures backing), and DataWhisper (already has 400+ paying customers). All three lead with natural language query as the primary interface.

Marcus: "This is the fastest the competitive landscape has shifted in the four years I've been here. We need to decide whether we're responding or leading."

Raj noted that our current NLQ feature is in beta with 12% adoption among existing users. Competitors are shipping NLQ as the default experience, not a secondary feature. The gap is widening monthly.

DECISION: Accelerate natural language query from Q3 launch to end of Q2. Raj to reallocate 4 engineers from the mobile team. Lisa to begin "Ask your data anything" messaging campaign in parallel.

2. MARKET SEGMENT FOCUS
Danielle presented pipeline data. Enterprise deals ($100K+ ACV) have a 22% win rate and 94-day average cycle. Mid-market ($25K-$100K) has a 34% win rate and 52-day cycle. SMB (<$25K) has a 41% win rate but only 8-day cycle and high churn.

The debate was heated. Lisa argued for doubling down on SMB with a self-serve motion and product-led growth. Danielle pushed for enterprise because "that's where the real revenue compounds." Raj sided with mid-market as the sweet spot — enough deal size to matter, short enough cycle to learn fast.

Marcus made the call: "We're a mid-market company that serves some enterprise accounts. We are not an SMB company. The unit economics don't work at SMB with our current cost structure."

DECISION: Primary focus mid-market ($25K-$100K ACV). Maintain enterprise accounts but don't expand the enterprise sales team. Evaluate SMB self-serve as a separate product line in Q4.

3. PRICING STRATEGY
Current pricing hasn't changed in 18 months. Competitors are underpricing by 20-30% on comparable features. Danielle flagged that 3 of her last 5 lost deals cited pricing as the primary reason.

Lisa proposed a "Starter" tier at $499/month to compete at the low end without cannibalizing the core product. Raj expressed concern about feature gating complexity. Marcus asked for a pricing committee to present options by April 30.

DECISION: No immediate price changes. Pricing committee (Danielle, Raj, Lisa) to present 3 options by end of April. Include analysis of impact on existing customer base.

4. CHURN ANALYSIS
Marcus raised the red flag: SMB churn hit 18% in Q1, up from 12% in Q4. Mid-market churn is stable at 5%. Enterprise churn is 2%.

Root causes identified by Raj: SMB customers don't complete onboarding (only 34% reach "first insight" within 30 days), they underutilize integrations, and they have no dedicated CSM.

Danielle: "We're acquiring SMB customers we can't retain. Every churned SMB customer costs us $1,200 in CAC we'll never recover."

DECISION: Immediate freeze on SMB marketing spend until the retention problem is diagnosed. Raj to implement an automated onboarding flow targeting the "first insight in 7 days" metric. Customer Success to flag any account that hasn't logged in for 14+ days.
