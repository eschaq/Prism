SALES CALL NOTES — Meridian Financial
Date: March 12, 2025
Prospect: Sarah Chen, VP of Operations, Meridian Financial
AE: David Park
Stage: Discovery / Qualification

BACKGROUND
Meridian Financial is a mid-market wealth management firm (~$2.4B AUM, 340 employees across 6 offices). Sarah reports to the COO and manages an operations analytics team of 8 analysts. They currently use a patchwork of Salesforce, Excel, an internal Oracle DB, Tableau Server, and a legacy reporting tool called FinView.

PAIN POINTS DISCUSSED
1. Manual reporting is killing productivity. Sarah's team spends approximately 3 full days per month pulling data from 5 different systems and reconciling it into executive reports. "We're spending the first week of every month just getting last month's numbers right."

2. Data is scattered everywhere. Client portfolio data lives in Oracle, sales pipeline in Salesforce, marketing attribution in HubSpot, financial actuals in NetSuite, and operational metrics in spreadsheets. No single source of truth exists. Different teams present different numbers in the same meetings.

3. Analyst time is wasted on data prep. Sarah estimated that her team of 8 analysts spends roughly 60% of their time on data gathering, cleaning, and formatting — leaving only 40% for actual analysis. "I hired analysts to find insights, not to copy-paste between spreadsheets."

4. Executive dashboards are always stale. By the time reports reach the C-suite, data is 2-3 weeks old. The CEO has started asking for "real numbers" because he doesn't trust the dashboards.

OBJECTIONS RAISED
- Budget: Sarah flagged that any new tool over $80K/year needs CFO sign-off. Their current analytics budget is ~$200K including Tableau licenses.
- IT approval: Meridian has a formal IT security review process that takes 4-6 weeks minimum. They require SOC 2 Type II and will need a security questionnaire completed.
- Timeline: Sarah wants something deployed before Q3 earnings prep (July 15). She's worried about implementation taking too long.
